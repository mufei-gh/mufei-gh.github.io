Tablet App

The New Kids
 Times
Research / UX / Visual Design / Program Testing
Project Duration: 3 months
Problem

News access is not friendly for kids in 4th grade through 8th grade who are willing to explore the world since news generally is from

adults' perspectives
with
hard-to-follow terms.
Also, there are
too many negativities
with in news magazines. Therefore, traditional news articles can be confusing and boring to read for kids and teenagers.

The project

Delivers a channel as an App on iPad that creates enjoyable experiences for kids to gain news literacy at any time.

Improvement

To improve users’ experience reading news, the team

attaches explanations to words
that exceed users’ reading level, web crawls news articles with
kids or teenagers related topics
and adds
more positive news
to the app. Moreover, the team creates a
“View” channel with opinion articles
for kids to know thoughts toward news topics from different people, with an interactive activity to submit their ideas through the article submission function.

Contribution

The News Kids Times is a course project designed and developed by a team of five. I was responsible for

every UI/UX-related job
(basically everything you see on this page) with front-end and back-end engineers. Besides, I implemented a few test cases on the front end and managed the project with a Gantt chart.

Research & Discovery
We did some media research asking questions below:

How do you describe news in a few words?
What do you think about the news?
Please tell me one of your experiences of reading the news.
Do you read the news often?
Is there anything that prevents you from reading the news?
Is there anything you think needs to be improved regarding current news articles?
What do you feel when you read the news?
Do you trust the news? If not, Why?

Then concluded the highlights of research findings.
Vocabulary Explanation

During the research, we found some users mentioned the news generally contain some hard-to-follow terms that they do not understand, and it is exhausted to check each of them manually. Therefore, we built the vocabulary explanation function that

enables users to view word explanations on the same page.
The vocabulary that exceeds 4th-grade reading level comes with an explanation.

CBC Kids News as a source of news

Some users are interested in reading news but feel upset because kids and teenagers, in general, are not involved in news articles. To encourage the interest, the team includes CBC Kids News as a source of the news posted in App,

balancing traditional news and kids-related topics.

More positive contents

The young audience would like to know important things happening with rational thinking, but many negativities in the news are still too heavy for them. Thus, the team decided to include more news articles with positive stories

balancing positive and critical facts on the back end.

"View" as one category

Some users said they feel ignored and bored because news articles are always from professional adults’ perspectives. The team builds a view channel with

opinion articles containing various perspectives
to improve the user experience. There are two sources of these opinion articles.

First, kids are encouraged to

submit their opinion articles
towards specific topics, then selected submissions will be under the “View” category. The team also hopes this interactive function can make the user experience more enjoyable.

Second, the team web crawled opinion articles from The New York Times, a reputable news source. Also, the team lists opinion articles under an individual category to help kids

discover the difference between news and opinion articles.
